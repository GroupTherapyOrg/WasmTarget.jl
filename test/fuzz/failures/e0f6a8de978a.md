## Analysis
**Root: ill-conditioned argument reduction (long tail, low value).** At x=100,
`x^x = 100.0^100.0 ≈ 1e200`, and the gap is `sin(1e200)`. `sin` of a huge argument
is catastrophically ill-conditioned: a 1-ULP difference in the `1e200` input
(`pow` via `exp(x·log x)` vs native `pow`) shifts the reduced angle by ~π, so the
results legitimately disagree (native=-0.644, wasm=0.276) even though both `pow`
and `sin` are individually near-correct. This isn't a soundness bug in the usual
sense — it's the well-known sensitivity of `sin`/`cos` near the top of the float
range, where the "right" answer depends on having the *exact* real value, which
neither side has after `^`. Lowest-priority; a closer-to-native `pow` (bit-exact
to Base's `Base.Math.^`) would shrink but not eliminate it.
