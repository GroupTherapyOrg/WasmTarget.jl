**Hypothesis:** a fixed/shared **scratch local** is reused without save/restore
across two vector ops evaluated in sequence. Trigger: a **left** operand that is a
vector reducer over a literal (`sum`/`prod`) **then** a **right** operand of the
form `maximum(sort(...))` — `maximum` reads the wrong slot after the left operand
wrote the shared scratch, returning the array *minimum*.

**Where to look:** `CompilationContext.scratch_locals` (allocated once, `context.jl`)
and the `sort` insertion-sort overlay + `maximum` reducer codegen — check for
overlapping scratch-local indices and missing spill across a sub-expression that
also touches those locals. The `+` vs `*` and order-sensitivity point at
operand-evaluation interleaving with shared mutable scratch.
