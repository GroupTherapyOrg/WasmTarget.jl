## Analysis
**Root cause: non-ASCII `Char` ops need Unicode tables (feature).** The trap is the
`uppercase('é')` sub-term (and `isdigit` on a non-ASCII char) — both route through
unimplemented Unicode case/category paths for codepoints ≥ 0x80. The `(0 == 0) & …`
wrapper and `isdigit` on ASCII are fine; the non-ASCII char is the trigger.

Same root as [[ff2542499cc2]] (`uppercase('é')`) and [[bc93362af34e]]
(`isspace('é')`). **Deferred to Part 2** (feature — Unicode tables).
